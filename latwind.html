Latitude vs. Wind Speed


After initial visual inspection of the graph, there does not seem to be a relationship between latitude and wind speed. The points are all scattered and there is no clear visual pattern. Linear regression was then performed to determine if a mathematical model could explain the results. The data was split into the northern hemisphere and southern hemisphere so that a more valid result could be obtained. The linear regression graphs are displayed below. The line equation is displayed on each graph.


Visualizations


Latitude vs. Wind Speed Linear Regression-Northern Hemisphere














The r-squared value is 0.01 which means there is not a relationship between latitude and wind speed in the northern hemisphere. This can also be determined by visually looking at the points on the plot, and notice that they are scattered about with no visually evident relationship.


Latitude vs. Wind Speed Linear Regression-Southern Hemisphere














The r-squared value is 0.2 which means there is not a relationship between latitude and wind speed in the southern hemisphere. This can also be determined by visually looking at the points on the plot, and notice that they are scattered about with no visually evident relationship.
